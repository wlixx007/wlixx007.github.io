My research interests:
  • Monte Carlo Methods for Big Data Analysis;
  • Large-Scale Linear Algebra;
  • High Performance Scientific Computing.

Linear algebra operations play a central role in scientific computing and data analysis. On one hand, the advent of modern high performance computing architectures with the increasing computing power has made it possible to deal with large volume of big data. One the other hand, most traditional, deterministic numerical algorithms for linear algebra operations scale poorly to handle big datasets, due to the low computational efficiency. Hence, performing large-scale linear algebra operations to efficiently extract valuable knowledge and insights from large-scale datasets is a difficult challenge.

The Monte Carlo methods, which are based on statistical sampling, exhibit many exclusively attractive properties when dealing with large volume of big data, including fast approximated results, memory efficiency, reduced data accesses, intrinsic parallelism, and inherent fault tolerance. Therefore, recently, there has been a large increase of interest of using Monte Carlo methods to carry out computations on large matrices.

In this work, we are proposing novel Monte Carlo methods to accommodate large-scale linear algebra operations across modern distributed and parallel computing architectures.
